Get your business or organization un‑stuck

Humanizing Data

Your organization needs to use data to support decision-making. But do you sometimes struggle to imagine how your data could be made humane, even pleasant to use? I'll work with you to help you develop ways to manage your data—ways that work for you and are comfortable for your team.

Mapping & Spatial Analysis

Do you need to map data with addresses, analyze clustering or hat maps, or decide where to locate a facility? Let's work together to humanize your spatial data and set up a system that will enable you to gain continuous insight from your maps, over the long term.

Dialogue Facilitation

Does an important decision need to be made, a problem solved creatively, or a critical conflict resolved? I'll work as a neutral facilitator, helping you create a decision-making process and keep it on track. Trained in facilitation at the Interaction Institute for Social Change, I'll help shepherd the process so that your organization can focus on making the right decisions.

Permit Expediting

What permits and licenses will you need to open your business (or keep it open), how much will it cost, and what do you need to do? Draw on years of experience working with cities' (often confusing) permitting processes: I'll gather requirements, ensure your paperwork is in order, and keep you updated on the status of all your permits.

Public servant, civic technology ace, peace-builder

In 2013, I joined the leadership team of Code for Boston, a Cambridge-based volunteer organization that works with public agencies in the Boston area and worked to solve challenges with technology solutions.


From 2013–2016, I developed online tools for the Metropolitan Area Planning Council—a regional planning agency serving 101 cities and towns in eastern Massachusetts—to inform and support both urban planners and the public.


While at MAPC, I trained at the Boston-based Interaction Institute for Social Change in Essential Facilitation and Fundamentals of Facilitation for Racial Justice Work.


In 2017 I began working for OpenCounter, analyzing & digitizing the zoning and permitting processes of 50+ cities, towns, and counties across the country. With OpenCounter, I developed proficiency in building clarity within (sometimes confusing) public processes.


In 2017 and 2018, I chaired my local neighborhood association, leading decision-making processes around contentious local issues, often involving new businesses.


In 2019, I graduated the New School of Yogic Arts yoga teacher training. This training included significant components of nonviolent communication, mindfulness, and trauma theory, all of which contribute to my approach to conflict resolution, decision-making, and relationships in general—both personal and business.
